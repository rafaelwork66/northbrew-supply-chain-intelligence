# Findings and Recommendations

## Executive finding
The main service risk is concentrated, not system-wide. The three highest-risk suppliers are
Supplier Q, Supplier D, Supplier I. Targeted supplier recovery should therefore precede
a broad inventory increase.

## Evidence
- Overall on-time delivery: 58.8%.
- Overall fill rate: 97.5%.
- Total landed purchasing cost: $1,305,183,706.
- Inventory stockout observations: 211.
- Highest stockout warehouse: Perth DC.

## Root cause
Late delivery risk rises during the fourth quarter and is disproportionately associated with
three suppliers. Inventory availability is also affected by static replenishment behaviour:
receipts occur intermittently while demand increases during November and December.

## Prioritised recommendations
1. **Quick win:** place the three highest-risk suppliers on a 90-day recovery plan with weekly
   OTIF, defect and lead-time reviews.
2. **Quick win:** apply seasonal reorder-point uplifts before October for high-demand SKUs.
3. **Strategic:** introduce supplier segmentation and dual sourcing for critical products.
4. **Strategic:** replace static reorder points with demand- and lead-time-based safety stock.
5. **Control:** reconcile dashboard KPIs monthly and investigate missing delivery confirmations.

## Limitation
The dataset is synthetic and the forecast extrapolates historical trend. Recommendations should
be piloted and monitored before full implementation.
